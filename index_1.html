Cryptocurrency

Cryptocurrency is a digital form of money that is used as a medium for online exchange. It uses a blockchain technology to operate publicly and connect all the users from all over the world. The domain is publicly open for the use of anyone from anywhere around the world. It is where the makers and takers can easily transfer money from one e-wallet to another.

Cryptocurrency is not only digital currency. It can also be utilised as an asset which can be sold between users in order to profit as the currency rates increase.

The value of Bitcoin has gradually increased since it was initially released in 2009. The value of one Bitcoin back in 2011 was only at $0.30 USD. Now, in 2019, the value of a Bitcoin has reached over $9,000 USD.

In fact, the highest recorded market value of a Bitcoin reached over $19,783.06 on December 17, 2017. The jump in the value of the cryptocurrency is definitely interesting. You can utilise the increasing market to your own benefit by observing the movement of its value and knowing the appropriate time to sell your asset for profit.

Before the existence of cryptocurrency, traditional remittance is usually done through a middleman. The role of the middleman is to connect the gap between sender and receiver for transactions to be done. However, the presence of a middleman brings high fees and slower transactions.

Due to the high transaction fees, a chunk of your asset can get taken away because of the need to pay for fees. The creation of Bitcoin gave a solution to the problem of double-spending. The miners who process transactions in remittances are compensated by the network itself. This means that users do not have to worry about paying extra fees.

Another problem solved by Bitcoin was the issue of loss information. Before, if one network loses information, it causes a chain reaction to the other networks. The creation of Bitcoin helped provide a solution.

Different types of Cryptocurrencies

Cryptocurrencies are now widely popular and are used as payment method for hotels, online purchases, wagering and more. Currently, there are many options that you can choose from, such as Bitcoin, Litecoin and Ethereum among others.

Bitcoin

What is Bitcoin?

Bitcoin was created by the person behind the pseudonym Satoshi Nakamoto in 2008. In 2009, the ledger was released and the first blockchain database was created. It solved the problem of double-spending in online remittances through the invention of a peer-to-peer network. Since then, Bitcoin has become the most used and known cryptocurrency in the world.

Cryptocurrency does sound intimidating, but you can easily use it once you know the basics of the process. It works just like the stock exchange with buyers and sellers, whereas Bitcoin has makers and takers.

Transaction Process

The transaction process begins with a maker wanting to send money through the network. This transaction will have to go through peer-to-peer in the database. The maker who will transfer money, shall make a transaction which needs to go through the network. The miners will complete the transaction by solving cryptographic puzzles and find a solution.

Then, the miner will confirm the transaction and send new information to everyone before the receiver will get the money. Each and every transaction must be updated to the database.

Advantages of Bitcoin

Decentralized network

Bitcoin doesnt have a central authority compared to fiat currency. Fiat currency refers to the local currency available in every country and is controlled by the government. In a centralized network, all records of accounts and balances are managed by the central server. However, the problem with this is that it results in double-spending.

Now, that there is the presence of Bitcoin. The cryptocurrency has its own exclusive network that keeps track of transaction records from peer to peer. Each peer of the network should have similar records of information for future purposes of verifying transactions.

Lower Fees

In Bitcoin, users will have to pay little to nothing when it comes to transaction fees. It is sure to be lower compared to traditional online remittance. The miners who accept the transactions receive their compensation from the network itself.

Moreover, transactions are typically not taxed. Now, bid farewell to the days when a part of your asset can get subtracted to serve as payment for transaction fees. On the other hand, you should keep in mind that the use of third-party apps to manage your e-wallet may cost other fees.

Safety and Security

Bitcoin features high levels of privacy and security of the database. This is to prevent crimes such as fraud and identity theft from happening. The network uses advanced security to protect information of users and the transactions made through the blockchain.

All transactions made in the network are done anonymously. This ensures the protection of the identity of all users. In fact, the transactions made in the database cannot be tracked and reversed.

After a complete transaction, there is no other way to retrieve it. Hence, users must be sure of the remittance before actually sending it. However, makers and takers can form a private agreement between them regarding the possibility of a refund.

Faster Transactions

Traditionally, transactions require the presence of brokers, agents, dealers or representatives. Then, the procedure will be followed by paperwork and processing of data. There is a lot of wasted time with the paperwork going from one person to another.

Thankfully, there is now the presence of Bitcoin. Since Bitcoin operates through a peer-to-peer network, the middleman is removed between the sender and receiver of each transaction. This ensures clarity in the sequence of transactions from the users of the network.

Publicly Available

Bitcoin uses a domain that is publicly available for anyone from anywhere around the world. The transparency of the database permits users to send and receive money globally. The peer-to-peer network database allows users to conduct a remittance and/or be a miner to process transactions.

Value Increases

Bitcoin was launched in 2009 and its value has increased rapidly since then. The price of a Bitcoin in 2011 was at 32.63 JPY. Currently, one Bitcoin is equivalent to over 1 million. On December 17, 2017, Bitcoin reached its highest value ever, at the price of 2,154,691.76.

The values of the cryptocurrency has remained stagnant and rises from time to time. It is certain that Bitcoin can be considered as an asset. The changing value of the digital currency is a game to play for users.

As the value of Bitcoin increases, it can serve as an asset which users can sell in order to profit. The public domain permits makers and takers to easily transfer money from one user to another. Litecoin

This cryptocurrency is a project fork of Bitcoin. It similarly works through a peer-to-peer network and is part of an open domain with public access for anyone to use. Litecoin started in October 2011 under the license of MIT/X11. It is also not managed by any central authority and transfers are done through the cryptographic protocol.

Ethereum

Ethereum is another decentralized network for online remittances. Just like the previous two, it is open to the public and is distributed through a blockchain technology. On July 30, 2015, Ethereum was initially launched to the public. This cryptocurrency was founded by five people namely Vitalik Buterin, Anthony Di Iorio, Charles Hoskinson, Mihai Alisie and Amir Chetrit.

Ripple

Simply known as XRP, Ripple is another digital currency exchange and remittance system that enables a fast way to send and receive money anywhere around the world through a blockchain technology. The cryptocurrency was created in 2012 by two individuals namely Arthur Britto and David Swartz. Then, it was finally launched by Ryan Fugger.

Bitcoin Cash

The cryptocurrency is another spin-off version of Bitcoin and was released in August 2017 by the elusive Satoshi Nakamoto. They decided to create an alteration to make the transactions per block even faster. Now, there is an assured transfer rate of 8MB to 32MB in comparison to Bitcoins 1MB per block.

What is a Bitcoin wallet?

It is a platform used to manage and store your bitcoins. There are different options when choosing which Bitcoin wallet you should use. There are some that are cloud-based, while others can be downloaded to your smartphone, PC, or tablet. Most Bitcoin wallets function the same way, but each has its own features.

The three types of Bitcoin wallets are the following:

Cloud Wallets are the easiest to use. On the other hand, putting your bitcoins in the cloud means giving the business holding your BTC the full responsibility of handling your money. We recommend Copay.io and Bitgo.com.

Software Wallets refer to the downloadable programs that you can use on your smartphone, tablet or computer. They are more secure compared to cloud wallets since you have total control.

However, it doesnt mean that there are no risks imposed. We recommend software wallets such as Copay.io, Breadwallet and Mycelium.

Hardware Wallets work by storing your private keys on a secure hardware device. The biggest advantage of this type of wallet is the immunity against computer viruses. The private keys are usually stored in a protected area of a microcontroller which means that they cannot be transferred out of the device. We recommend hardware wallets such as Trezor and Ledger.
